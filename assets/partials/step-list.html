How to start

Step 1. Get approval to begin

Speak with your manager or agency delegate to seek in-principle support to proceed.

Your manager or delegate needs to consider a number of matters. These include:

  • budget
  • changes to other jobs
  • what parts of the job are new or no longer being carried out.

Step 2. Write the JAQ

Use the JAQ guideline to guide you through each question in the JAQ.

Choose one of the following templates:

  • for positions under AO4 or equivalent, you can use the brief JAQ template
  • all positions above AO4 or equivalent must use the JAQ template.

Accuracy of a job evaluation outcome depends on the quality of the information provided in a JAQ.

Focus on the following types of information:

  • significant tasks that fall within the responsibility of the role
  • activities that fill a normal work day, week or year
  • the minimum level of knowledge, skills or qualifications required.

The person writing the JAQ must describe the job in a succinct and impersonal manner.

Step 3. Request approval to evaluate

Once you have written the JAQ, you can request to have the position evaluated or benchmarked.
